# SKILLS.md

This file defines reusable workflows for contributors working on the content and presentation of `kyleorman.com`.

Each skill should produce public-safe, accurate, employer-friendly output.

## Skill: Professional bio writing

**When to use**

- Writing or revising a short, medium, or long professional bio

**Inputs needed**

- Current role or focus
- Experience highlights
- Academic status and research direction
- Target length

**Workflow**

1. Identify the single clearest professional positioning statement
2. Select 2-4 supporting details from experience, projects, or academic work
3. Keep the opening readable for employers who may only skim once
4. Include academic context without letting it dominate unless the page requires it
5. Remove filler, hype, and repetition

**Expected output**

- A concise bio that sounds credible, warm, and specific

**Avoid**

- Empty adjectives like "passionate" without evidence
- Long autobiographical tangents
- Overclaiming expertise or impact

## Skill: Achievement normalization

**When to use**

- Converting resume, CV, or notes into site-ready bullets

**Inputs needed**

- Raw achievement text
- Dates, organizations, and roles
- Any measurable outcomes

**Workflow**

1. Rewrite each item into a consistent format
2. Lead with the action, result, or recognition
3. Preserve context needed for non-specialists
4. Keep bullets short enough to scan quickly
5. Check that every achievement is accurate and current

**Expected output**

- Clean, consistent bullets suitable for public presentation

**Avoid**

- Resume jargon without explanation
- Duplicating the same achievement across sections without purpose
- Inflating routine responsibilities into major accomplishments

## Skill: Academic summary for mixed audiences

**When to use**

- Summarizing PhD work, research interests, lab experience, publications, or presentations

**Inputs needed**

- Research area
- Role and contribution
- Publication or presentation details
- Audience context

**Workflow**

1. Explain the research area in plain language first
2. State the specific contribution accurately
3. Clarify publication status, venue, and authorship if relevant
4. Emphasize significance without overstating prominence
5. Keep publications and research nested under Education & Research unless the project direction changes later

**Expected output**

- A clear research summary that is understandable to employers and respectful of academic nuance

**Avoid**

- Assuming readers know the field-specific importance automatically
- Presenting co-authored work as solo work
- Giving publications more weight than the evidence supports

## Skill: Project summary writing

**When to use**

- Turning technical work into portfolio entries or featured project blurbs

**Inputs needed**

- Project goal
- Stack or tools used
- Your contribution
- Result, impact, or lesson learned

**Workflow**

1. Start with what the project is and why it matters
2. Add the most relevant technical detail, not every implementation detail
3. Clarify your personal role
4. End with an outcome, capability demonstrated, or takeaway
5. Keep the summary readable to both engineers and employers

**Expected output**

- A compact project summary with clear relevance

**Avoid**

- Tool lists without context
- Dense architecture detail in overview copy
- Hiding the outcome behind technical description

## Skill: Employer scan optimization

**When to use**

- Reviewing a page or section for fast comprehension by hiring managers

**Inputs needed**

- Draft page copy
- Section order
- Key messages to preserve

**Workflow**

1. Check whether the first screen communicates role, focus, and value clearly
2. Move the strongest evidence higher on the page
3. Tighten headings and bullet phrasing for skimmability
4. Remove anything that feels vague, repetitive, or distracting
5. Confirm that a quick reader can understand the profile in under a minute

**Expected output**

- Improved structure and copy for high-speed review

**Avoid**

- Burying experience under long introductions
- Making readers infer the main story
- Treating every detail as equally important

## Skill: Accessibility and copy QA

**When to use**

- Reviewing content before publication

**Inputs needed**

- Final or near-final copy

**Workflow**

1. Check heading clarity and logical structure
2. Check sentence length and readability
3. Replace ambiguous link labels and vague references
4. Remove avoidable jargon or define it clearly
5. Review for grammar, spelling, and formatting consistency

**Expected output**

- Cleaner, clearer, more accessible content

**Avoid**

- Dense blocks of text
- Unexplained acronyms
- Clever wording that reduces clarity

## Skill: SEO metadata drafting

**When to use**

- Drafting page titles, descriptions, and social preview copy

**Inputs needed**

- Page purpose
- Primary audience
- Key terms that naturally fit the content

**Workflow**

1. Write a straightforward page title
2. Draft a restrained description that matches visible page content
3. Keep naming consistent across metadata and headings
4. Avoid keyword stuffing or exaggerated claims

**Expected output**

- Metadata that is accurate, readable, and aligned with the page

**Avoid**

- Clickbait phrasing
- Generic descriptions that could apply to anyone
- Mismatch between metadata and actual page content

## Skill: Fact and link verification

**When to use**

- Before publishing updated profile content

**Inputs needed**

- Claims, links, dates, titles, and affiliations to review

**Workflow**

1. Check every date and role title
2. Confirm organization and institution names
3. Verify links resolve correctly and point to the intended destination
4. Confirm publication and presentation details are presented accurately
5. Flag anything uncertain for manual confirmation

**Expected output**

- A set of verified facts and flagged uncertainties

**Avoid**

- Guessing missing details
- Preserving stale links because they are "close enough"
- Publishing uncertain claims without qualification
