# AGENTS.md

## Project overview

This repository contains the source and project documentation for `kyleorman.com`, a public-facing professional website for Kyle Orman.

The site should present professional and academic work in a way that is credible, polished, and easy for potential employers to review quickly.

## Audience priority

1. Employers, recruiters, and hiring managers
2. Technical peers and collaborators
3. Academic readers

When tradeoffs arise, optimize first for clarity and credibility with employers while preserving enough technical and academic depth to support deeper review.

## Primary goals

- Present a strong, accurate professional profile
- Highlight relevant experience, projects, and achievements
- Show academic progress and research interests without overstating them
- Keep the site approachable, readable, and appropriate for a broad public audience

## Non-goals

- Overly casual personal branding
- Gimmicky copy or design direction
- Inflated claims, vague superlatives, or unverifiable impact statements
- Sharing sensitive personal information

## Content priorities

Content should generally emphasize the following, in order:

1. Professional experience and practical impact
2. Technical strengths and selected projects
3. Education, PhD trajectory, and research context
4. Awards, honors, publications, presentations, and related achievements

## Preferred site structure

Unless there is a strong reason to change it, use this information architecture:

- Home / landing summary
- About
- Experience
- Education & Research
  - Publications and research outputs should live within this section, not as a separate top-level section for now
- Selected Projects
- Achievements
- Skills
- Contact

## Tone and voice

Use a professional-warm tone:

- Clear and confident
- Concise and specific
- Approachable, but not casual
- Factual rather than promotional

Prefer plain language over jargon. When technical or academic terms are necessary, make them understandable to a non-specialist reader.

## Writing guidance

- Prefer short paragraphs, strong headings, and scannable bullets
- Lead with outcomes, responsibilities, and areas of focus
- Use measurable impact when it is available and accurate
- Translate technical work into employer-readable value
- Translate academic work into clear relevance and scope
- Keep tense and formatting consistent within each section

## Accuracy rules

- Verify dates, titles, affiliations, roles, links, and names
- Do not imply first-author or lead-author weight when that is not true
- Describe research and publications accurately and proportionally
- Do not overstate ownership, novelty, or impact
- If a claim cannot be supported, remove or soften it

## Public-facing safety rules

- Do not publish private contact details beyond what is intentionally meant to be public
- Do not include confidential work details from employers, labs, or collaborators
- Avoid internal-only terminology that needs outside context to make sense
- Assume every page may be read by employers, colleagues, and the public

## Accessibility and UX expectations

- Make content easy to skim
- Use descriptive headings and meaningful link text
- Avoid dense walls of text
- Preserve semantic structure and logical heading order
- Prefer clarity over cleverness

## SEO and metadata guidance

- Keep page titles and descriptions accurate and restrained
- Use consistent naming for Kyle Orman across the site
- Favor straightforward section names over clever labels
- Ensure summary text reflects the actual content of the page

## Repository documentation expectations

- Keep `README.md` aligned with the current project status
- Keep `SKILLS.md` practical and workflow-oriented
- Update guidance documents when the site's purpose, audience, or structure changes materially

## Definition of done for content changes

A content change is ready when it is:

- Factually accurate
- Appropriate for public viewing
- Consistent with the professional-warm tone
- Useful to employers on a quick scan
- Understandable to both technical and non-technical readers
- Free of obvious grammar, spelling, and formatting issues
